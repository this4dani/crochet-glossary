From Curious Student to Pattern Problem Solver

It started when a teacher introduced me to computers. Something just clicked. By high school, I was playing with Python and random number games, fascinated by how code could make computers respond.

Then came the MySpace era. I had to have the coolest page, which meant learning HTML and becoming obsessed with hex codes. Little did I know that obsession would lead to building tools for crochet patterns decades later.

I was a hands-on learner who learned best by doing, so I taught myself everything through trial and error. After 30 years of crocheting and countless hours rewriting patterns for simple adjustments, I realized there had to be a better way.
