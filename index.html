From 8-Year-Old Beginner to 40-Year-Old Problem Solver

It started in middle school with an teacher who introduced me to computers. Something just clicked. By high school, I was playing with Python and random number games.

Then came the MySpace era. I had to have the coolest page, which meant learning HTML and becoming obsessed with hex codes. Little did I know that obsession would lead to building AI for crochet patterns decades later.

I was a hands-on learner who learned best by doing, so I taught myself everything through trial and error. Turns out, being curious and stubborn about figuring things out is exactly what's needed to solve real-world problems.
